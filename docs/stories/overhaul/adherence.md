# Der Wochen-Pillenplan: Halt den Rhythmus — Torso-first redesign

_Story id: `adherence`_

> **You hand-build Lena's whole week with zero time pressure, then turn the days over ONE TAP AT A TIME and watch the slow pump carve your own plan into the torso as a living saw-tooth — every gap you left treads the water down, and when a missed day leaves it low, YOU decide live on the tank whether one calm pill breathes it back to green or a panicky "double to catch up" climbs the wrong way toward the red tape.**

## Signature mechanic

COMPOSE-A-WEEK, THEN TURN THE DAYS BY HAND: the player authors a 7-slot Mo–So plan with ZERO time pressure (empty = vergessen, double = nachholen are physically buildable), then advances playback ONE VISITOR TAP PER DAY ("Nächster Tag") so every single driveTo is triggered by their hand on the exhibit — never an idle cutscene. The plan IS the curve and the visitor pulls it through the body day by day. This is the only time-axis authoring verb in the set: compose-then-hand-crank, the deliberate opposite of Johanniskraut's react-under-pressure leak defence. Within playback sits the one live hypothesis-test the grammar wants: when a gap leaves the water low, the player taps a fix ON the tank and the WATER answers (single = slow heal into green; double = wrong-way climb toward red), and tapping the obviously-wrong "stop the pill entirely" makes the pump NOT move at all — stillness as the instant "that's not the danger here" reply.

## The torso is the star

Nothing on screen ever shows Lena's level — the screen holds the calendar strip during authoring and, during playback, only a day-ticker (Mo… Di…) and a big "Schau auf den Körper" arrow; the real tank is the sole readout. During authoring the tank sits dead-still at the steady-state ~62 in the green band, so a held rhythm literally LOOKS like calm water before a single day plays. Then each day the visitor turns becomes a real, visitor-triggered driveTo whose MOVE SIZE and a held beat at the extreme carry the drama (not an unreachable rate): a correct day barely moves (a small breath that settles right back, reading as 'held'); a forgotten day treads the surface visibly DOWN one full step and the next-day tap waits for the visitor; a 'catch-up' double crawls the water UP and is made to PAUSE for ~1s sitting near the red tape before the day clears — the visitor feels the danger sit on the glass. The payoff scene is purely water travelling: on the trap plan the surface climbs toward 80 and the visitor sees the loss coming a full second before the engine freezes the curve. The saw-tooth lives on the glass, never on a chart or bar.

## Stillness moment (stillness = information)

Two distinct stillness uses, both load-bearing. (1) AUTHORING stillness: the player builds the entire week against a calm, motionless tank parked at ~62 in the green — so 'kept the rhythm' is taught as non-moving water before any day plays, and a correct play-day later is near-stillness (a small breath that settles back) = the answer 'gehalten / harmlos'. (2) STILLNESS-AS-ANSWER in the live decision: when the water sits low after a gap, one of the offered taps is the wrong-model panic move 'Tablette ganz weglassen / abwarten'; tapping it makes the pump NOT move — an instant, motionless 'das ist nicht die Lösung' that the visitor reads off the glass, exactly the tap-a-wrong-idea-and-the-body-stays-still pattern. Deviation (a gap fall, or a double's wrong-way climb) is the only real travel; stillness = 'das ist fein / das ist nicht es'.

## Read-the-body decision

It is now a LIVE intervention, not a pause on a plan you already committed. The authored plan deliberately leaves Tuesday's slot for the engine to mark forgotten (or the player intentionally left a gap); when Wednesday is turned, playback stops with the water sitting visibly BELOW the green band (~54) and the screen asks 'Was jetzt? Schau, wo das Wasser steht.' With NO number shown, the player reads the tank — it dipped but is well clear of the red floor — and chooses among three taps that the BODY answers: 'Heute EINE Tablette, normal weiter' breathes the water slowly back up into the green (~62, heals); 'Morgen ZWEI, nachholen' crawls the surface the WRONG way, up and OVER the band, pausing alarmingly near the red tape before the day clears (overshoot, not heal); 'die Pille heute ganz weglassen' makes the pump NOT move at all (instant 'nicht es'). Same on-screen buttons; the right answer is dictated purely by where the water is relative to the band, read off the glass.

## The twist

The counterintuitive body move, made honest and tank-native: 'catching up makes it WORSE, not better — the water climbs the WRONG way.' The visitor's intuition is that a double dose repairs a missed day; on the tank the double does the opposite of healing — the surface travels UP toward the danger tape while a plain single pill is what actually restores the green. The sharper, ARITHMETICALLY HONEST surprise (the old 'same gesture safe once / fatal next by luck' framing is CUT because it taught false state-dependence and had no path in the sim): a 'catch-up' double is never neutral — from a low post-gap level it overshoots out of the band (visibly wrong, survives), but from an already-FULL level it crosses the red tape and auto-loses. The lesson the water teaches is additive and true — more drug always pushes UP, so doubling onto a body that is already topped up is what tips it over, and the only safe move after any gap is the single calm pill.

## End states

- **win** — Authored exactly one pill every day (clean 7×1), OR left a single forgotten day and answered the live read-the-body decision with the plain single pill so the tank breathed back into the green; turned the whole week to So and it ended settled in the band.
  - _Torso:_ Resting calm in the green band ~62 — a flat-topped saw-tooth; the body kept its rhythm all week. The proof of 'never double' is the water, not a quiz.
- **over** — A 'catch-up' double that drives the crest across the red tape from an already-full body — the deterministic trap (two doubles 62→78→94, or a double answered at the live decision when the body is already full). +16 per double makes the overshoot read hard and the full-body double trip reliably.
  - _Torso:_ Frozen at ≥80, surface at the red high tape — playback halted mid-crawl on the doubling day by the simulated trajectory; remaining days never played.
- **under** — Too many consecutive forgotten days with no catch-up — the staircase 62→54→46→38→30 crosses the low line on the 4th gap.
  - _Torso:_ Frozen at ≤35 near the floor — the protective level fell away (breakthrough-seizure picture); curve stopped on the gap day that broke it.

## Learnings — young

- Jeden Tag genau EINE Tablette hält den Schutz gleichmäßig — schau, wie ruhig das Wasser im Grünen bleibt.
- Eine vergessene Tablette holt man NICHT mit zwei nach — einfach normal mit der einen weitermachen; zwei auf einmal lassen das Wasser nach oben zur roten Linie steigen, nicht zurück ins Grüne.
- Zu oft vergessen, und der Schutz sackt weg — das Wasser fällt Stufe für Stufe nach unten.

## Learnings — adult

- Lamotrigin schützt nur bei KONSTANTEM Wirkspiegel — die Regelmäßigkeit selbst ist die Therapie, nicht die einzelne Dosis.
- Eine verpasste Dosis NICHT verdoppeln: 'Nachholen' treibt den Spiegel in die falsche Richtung (dosisabhängige Toxizität — Ataxie, Doppelbilder, Sedierung); normal mit der Einzeldosis weiterfahren. Mehr Wirkstoff schiebt den Spiegel immer nach OBEN — auf einen vollen Körper draufgesetzt kippt er über die Schwelle.
- Wiederholte Auslassungen senken den Spiegel unter das Fenster → Durchbruchsanfall; Non-Adhärenz ist eine Hauptursache. Nach einer LÄNGEREN Pause neu eintitrieren statt sofort volle Dosis (Risiko schwerer Hautreaktionen, SJS/TEN) — FI-gegengeprüft vor dem Open Day.

## Scoring

Stars 0–3 in 0.5 steps via the engine's starsFor(win, clever, pro). Any loss (over/under) = 0 stars. A win = 1 base star ('Geschützt': week ended in the green band). CLEVER bonus (0/0.5/1): full 1.0 for a 'Sauberer Plan' — exactly one pill in all seven slots on the FIRST run, no empty slots, no doubles, no retry (the calm flat saw-tooth); 0.5 for a win that needed a recovered gap or a retry. PRO bonus (0/0.5/1) now earned ON THE TANK, not by a quiz: full 1.0 for answering the live read-the-body decision correctly on the FIRST tap (the single calm pill that heals the water into green) — i.e. the visitor proved 'nicht verdoppeln' by water travel; 0.5 if they tapped a wrong option first (the no-move 'weglassen' or the wrong-way double) before correcting. So a flawless run (clean 7×1, no gap to decide) auto-earns the full pro = 3.0 'SafePolyMed-Meister'/'Klinische:r Pharmazeut:in'; a win with one recovered gap answered right first-try ~2.5; a sloppy-but-survived week ~1.5–2.0; any overdose/underdose = 0. The closing rule-quiz is DELETED as a star decider; the adult re-titrate/SJS-TEN nuance becomes a one-line debrief fact on the EndScreen, not a graded multiple-choice.

## Distinctness

This is the ONLY compose-then-hand-crank / time-axis authoring verb: the player builds a whole sequence with zero time pressure, then turns it through the body one deliberate tap per day. It is the deliberate opposite of Johanniskraut's react-under-pressure real-time leak defence (the closest neighbour — kept maximally contrasted by having NO timer or prompting anywhere in authoring; only the visitor's own taps pace playback). The signature object is the 7-slot calendar strip; the signature FEELING is 'watch your own plan become the body's curve, and pull it through by hand.' The previously least-distinct beat — a generic verbal multiple-choice rule-quiz that graded the pro star, the exact pattern the whole redesign is killing across all six stories — is now DELETED; the lesson is proven on the tank, so the ending is as distinct as the verb. The signature also stays on the SAW-TOOTH SHAPE (the up-and-down, especially the doubling SPIKES upward toward the red tape); Johanniskraut owns the pure monotone-downward drain, so a visitor who plays both never sees the same tank picture.
