# Die Blut-Balance — Torso-first redesign

_Story id: `ddi`_

> **Aim one interaction-scanner at an old man's pill plan and let the real tank answer — four pairings leave the water dead still (the body shrugs), but the new antibiotic plus his blood-thinner makes the water climb on its own toward the red bleeding line, and now you have to read how high it sits and talk it back into green without tipping it across either red tape.**

## Signature mechanic

SCAN-A-PAIRING -> THE TANK ANSWERS (stillness-vs-surge). The player locks ONE fixed probe (the new antibiotic) and taps it against each existing pill in turn; the verb is "scan this pairing and watch the body." Four harmless pairings make the pump hold DEAD STILL (a deliberate, held, non-moving level = "vertragen sich") and the blood-thinner pairing — gated to LAST so everyone feels still·still·still·still·SURGE — makes the water climb upward on its own. The tank IS the interaction alarm: no on-screen richtig/falsch box, the water's stillness or rise is the entire verdict.

## The torso is the star

The torso is "Herr Schmidts Blut-Balance" end to end, and every drama beat is water moving, never an emoji. It rises 40->62 on entry as a living green baseline. In the meaning-teach it is nudged a hair toward the high tape ("zu dünn") and a hair toward the low tape ("zu wenig Schutz") so both red lines are learned by watching water, not by reading a quiz. In the scanner it is the answer key: held flat for four pairings, a self-driven climb for the dangerous one. The twist is shown ON the body to the whole crowd — "er gibt aus Vorsicht Magenschutz dazu" and the water climbs ANYWAY, proving the safe pill changed nothing. The water is left sitting visibly HIGH and the screen never says how high — the read-the-body decision is made by looking at the real tank versus the two red tapes. Wrong corrections drive the water across a red tape for an instant, mid-travel AUTO-TRIP loss (rebuilt manually, JkPlay-style); the only body-correct plan glides it back to a steady green hold, and that calm still-green water is the win and the proof.

## Stillness moment (stillness = information)

The interaction scanner (Stage 3) is the signature stillness beat: scanning the antibiotic against Herz-Mittel, Blutdruck-Mittel, Zucker-Mittel and Magenschutz each holds the pump DELIBERATELY STILL — a pure setTimeout hold (~1.4 s) with no driveTo call, under a big on-screen cue "Schau auf den Körper — bewegt sich das Wasser?" then "Nichts bewegt sich. Die zwei vertragen sich." Non-movement IS the "harmlos / not it" answer, and a wrong scan costs nothing. A second stillness beat seeds the lesson in Stage 2: dropping the new pill into the plan holds the tank flat ("noch nichts kombiniert — der Körper hat nicht reagiert"), priming the contrast. Only the blood-thinner pairing breaks the stillness with a slow, suspenseful self-climb.

## Read-the-body decision

Stage 5 (strategy) is read purely off the real water, never narrated. After the twist demo the tank is left sitting visibly HIGH (held ~76, just under the red tape) and the screen says ONLY "Schau auf den Körper. Was ist jetzt sicher?" — it does NOT tell the player the water is high. The player must look at the physical tank, see it near the top red tape, and reason: "it's up near the bleeding line, so the danger right now is too-thin blood; the fix has to bring it DOWN into green — but not so far it crosses the bottom red tape." Reading "it's high" is what rules out 'stop the thinner' (which yanks it DOWN through the low red tape = clot) and rules out 'just take both / only stomach-protection / raise the antibiotic dose' (which shove it further UP through the high red tape = bleed). Only the choice that reads the body — check the whole plan with pharmacy/doctor — settles the water back into the green band. No INR number is ever shown; the verdict is the water's position relative to the two red tapes.

## The twist

The Magenschutz (stomach-protection) trap, made UNAVOIDABLE by moving it into the demo. Every player has just watched stomach-protection sit dead-still and safe in the scanner. The Stage-4 demo is then framed for the crowd as "Herr Schmidt is nervous about his stomach, so out of caution he just adds the stomach-protection pill" — and the water keeps climbing UPWARD on its own anyway (62->76), because protecting the stomach does nothing to lower the blood-thinner effect. The counterintuitive body move: a pill you literally just proved was "safe" fails to save him; "more protection" does nothing to the real problem. Because the twist is now the mandatory demo (not one hidden option), every single player witnesses "the safe pill changed nothing on the body." The loss copy frames it as the untreated INTERACTION rising, never as the stomach-pill harming him, so it never teaches PPI=dangerous.

## End states

- **win** — Stage 5 safe strategy ('Ganzen Plan prüfen — Apotheke/Arztpraxis fragen') AND completing the Stage 6 safe-plan sort. Water settled back into the green band at 62.
  - _Torso:_ Held steady in the green band ~62 with a gentle confident pulse — calm, balanced blood. Title 'Sicher kombiniert!'.
- **over** — Stage 5 dangerous high picks: 'einfach beides nehmen' (->90), 'nur Magenschutz dazu' (->86, the twist echoed), or adult-only 'Antibiotikum-Dosis selbst reduzieren' (->88). Water driven UP across the red high tape; manual auto-trip the instant it crosses 80.
  - _Torso:_ Frozen at the top: water has crossed the red HIGH line — blood too thin, Blutungsrisiko. Young: 'Die Blut-Balance ist zu hoch — das Blut ist zu dünn, Blutungen werden gefährlich.' Adult: 'INR/Antikoagulation zu hoch -> Blutungsrisiko. Die Wechselwirkung wurde nicht behandelt.'
- **under** — Stage 5 'Blutverdünner aus Angst weglassen' / 'Phenprocoumon eigenmächtig absetzen' (->30). Water driven DOWN across the red low tape; manual auto-trip the instant it crosses 35.
  - _Torso:_ Frozen at the bottom: water has dropped through the red LOW line — too little protection, clot/stroke risk. Young: 'Zu wenig Schutz — gefährliche Blutklumpen können entstehen.' Adult: 'VKA eigenmächtig abgesetzt -> Verlust des Schlaganfallschutzes -> Thrombose-/Embolierisiko.'

## Learnings — young

- Eine Wechselwirkung entsteht oft, wenn zwei Mittel zusammenkommen — hier neues Antibiotikum + Blutverdünner.
- Das neue Antibiotikum macht den Blutverdünner STÄRKER: die Blut-Balance steigt zu hoch.
- Zu viel Blutverdünnung = Blutungsgefahr (zu dünnes Blut). Zu wenig = gefährliche Blutklumpen. Beides ist riskant.
- Magenschutz schützt den Magen — aber er senkt die Blut-Balance nicht. Er löst dieses Problem nicht.
- Darum immer den GANZEN Medikamentenplan zeigen und Apotheke oder Arztpraxis fragen, bevor man etwas Neues nimmt — und den Blutverdünner nie allein absetzen.

## Learnings — adult

- Clarithromycin kann über mehrere Abbau-/Transportwege die Phenprocoumon-Wirkung erhöhen; klinisch relevant ist der INR-Anstieg über Tage.
- Die Interaktion erhöht die Antikoagulation -> Blutungsrisiko; die Richtung ist 'zu stark', nicht 'zu schwach'.
- Eigenmächtiges Absetzen des VKA ist ebenfalls gefährlich: Verlust des Schlaganfallschutzes, Thrombose-/Embolierisiko. Zwei-seitige Gefahr.
- Ein PPI/Magenschutz senkt den INR nicht — Symptomschutz ersetzt kein Interaktionsmanagement. Der PPI ist neutral, nicht schädlich; gefährlich ist die unbehandelte Wechselwirkung.
- Zentraler Apotheken-Schritt: Cross-Check jeder neuen Verordnung gegen den gesamten Bestand; interaktionsärmere Antibiose mit der Ärztin/dem Arzt besprechen ODER engmaschige INR-Kontrolle mit ärztlicher Dosisanpassung.

## Scoring

Win = 1.0 base star (Stage 5 safe strategy + Stage 6 sort completed). On top, FOUR independent 0.5 flags summed inline JkPlay-style — `starCount = win ? 1 + [scanClean, twistRead, strategyFirstTry, sortClean].filter(Boolean).length * 0.5 : 0` — giving 1.0–3.0 in 0.5 steps, NOT routed through stars()'s two-bonus contract. The four flags: (1) scanClean — scanned ALL FOUR harmless pairings before the blood-thinner surge (the gating guarantees the order, so this flag rewards not skipping any stillness, i.e. fully experiencing the contrast; pedagogy and incentive now agree). (2) twistRead — after the Stage-4 demo, on a single quick confirm tap the player acknowledges the water rose (no longer a redundant 'stärker?' quiz — it is a one-tap 'I saw the water climb' confirm; right first time = flag). (3) strategyFirstTry — Stage 5 safe strategy chosen first, no dangerous pick attempted first (any dangerous pick auto-trips to loss anyway, so this flag really rewards going straight to the body-correct read). (4) sortClean — Stage 6 safe-plan sort has zero wrong placements. ANY Stage-5 dangerous pick = manual auto-trip loss = 0 stars, loss rank only. Clever half (scan + twist) = detective work; pro half (strategy + sort) = clean management.

## Distinctness

This story OWNS 'aim ONE fixed probe across many pills and read stillness-vs-single-surge off the body.' The player holds the new antibiotic as a fixed scanner and the answer is the tank's non-movement for four pairings versus one self-climb. That is distinct from the JK week-investigation (a time-axis multi-day DRAIN you watch accumulate while collecting history clues — falling creep over days, not a pairing scan), from gene's same-pill-three-different-results, from organ's dose titration (here the dose is fixed and correct from the start — there is no 'find the right dose'), and from any sort-only mechanic (the single Stage-6 sort is a supporting check, not the signature). Distinctness is deliberately concentrated in Stages 3–5 (scan -> twist demo -> read-the-body) which now dominate the runtime; the generic back half is cut to ONE sort so the unique verb is not diluted. The Stage-5 choose-and-drive shape exists in other stories, but here it is gated behind a true READ-THE-BODY (the screen withholds the level), which the legacy DdiPlay/gene/organ choice beats do not do.

## Buildability

Maps onto the REAL v2 engine, verified against the code. CRITICAL FIX applied: auto-trip does NOT exist on the v2 path (game.svelte.ts gates the critical-zone trip behind the legacy PLAY_PHASES; v2 stories run via driveTo()->extWait with no critical-zone check — the shipped DdiPlay drives to 88/92/28 and only sets outcome on SETTLE, so the pump fully crawls with no mid-travel trip). The redesign REBUILDS auto-trip manually exactly like JkPlay.svelte:71-81: a Svelte `$effect` watching `game.level.level` during the Stage-5 decision move that flips `beat='outcome'` with the right Outcome ('over' if >=80, 'under' if <=35) the instant the water crosses a red tape — so the loss resolves mid-travel as designed. The design no longer claims the engine auto-trips on its own. Other reuse is genuine: driveTo(target, rate, onSettle) for every move; EndScreen with score=starCount and outcome exactly as DdiPlay/JkPlay; inline `1 + flags.filter(Boolean)*0.5` star sum copied from JkPlay (NOT stars()'s two-bonus contract). The Stage-3 scanner is the existing PairLink refactored into 'tap a pill to scan it against the locked new drug' (tap-tap, kiosk-safe), with the blood-thinner tile disabled until the other four are scanned. Stage 6 reuses the existing DDI_FINALE bin-sort. STILLNESS is buildable as a pure component setTimeout hold (no driveTo) with a strong on-screen 'nichts bewegt sich' cue so a walk-up reads non-movement as an answer, not lag — the screen carries the cue precisely because the still water cannot. The forbidden Stage-4 'red ghost-marker drawn on the tank' is DROPPED (it was either an on-screen mirrored vessel — banned by grammar rule 2, and v2 has no on-screen tank — or un-drawable runtime tape); the player's eyes go to the REAL red tape instead. HARDWARE-PACING: scanner surge 62->72 ~4 s, demo 72->76 ~1.3 s, win glide 76->62 ~2.8 s; the worst loss plunge 76->30 trips at 35 after ~7 s at rate 6, all others trip within ~2-3 s. Tune scanner-hold (~1.4 s) and loss rates on the bench. No on-screen vessel/meter anywhere (the JK 'min' chip is deliberately NOT copied — eyes stay on the hardware).

## Copy tone

Two registers via game.ageGroup like every story. YOUNG: warm, concrete — 'Blutverdünner', 'neues Antibiotikum', 'Blut-Balance', 'Blutwert'; never INR/Phenprocoumon/Clarithromycin; concrete consequences (Nasenbluten, blaue Flecken, Blutklumpen). Encouraging on wrong scans ('Schau nochmal — diese zwei machen keinen Alarm'). ADULT: precise but not Fachinformation-overloaded — real names as sublabels (Phenprocoumon/Marcumar, Clarithromycin, INR/Antikoagulation); soft mechanism ('über mehrere Abbau-/Transportwege', 'kann die Phenprocoumon-Wirkung erhöhen', 'INR-Anstieg über Tage'); AVOID 'v.a. CYP3A4-metabolisiert'. AVOID implying the rise is instantaneous — frame it as 'in den nächsten Tagen / am nächsten Tag steigt das Wasser', honouring the multi-day PK reality while the slow pump shows the direction. SEND EYES TO THE HARDWARE everywhere: scanner 'Schau auf den Körper — bewegt sich das Wasser?', demo 'Schau, das Wasser steigt trotzdem Richtung roter Linie', strategy 'Schau auf den Körper. Was ist jetzt sicher?' (NEVER 'das Wasser steht hoch' — the player must read it). The screen never shows a verdict box; the tank shows it. ## Open questions

- Scanner stillness duration: ~1.4 s held-still proposed (a touch longer than the old 1.2 s so a walk-up clearly registers 'it did not move'). Needs a quick bench read on the real slow pump to confirm it reads as 'answer' not 'frozen/lag' — the on-screen 'nichts bewegt sich' cue must appear immediately on tap to cover the gap.
- Bench pacing for the 76->30 'stop the thinner' plunge: at rate 6 the manual trip fires when it crosses 35 (~7 s of travel). Verify on hardware that ~7 s reads as suspense, not dead air; bump to rate 7 if needed. All UP losses trip within ~2-3 s so they are fine.
- Twist legibility: confirm the Stage-4 demo framing ('he added the safe stomach-pill and the water rose anyway') lands as 'the safe pill did nothing to the real problem' for a walk-up child, not as 'the stomach-pill is bad.' A copy pass with a non-expert reader is worth doing.
- Stage-1 meaning-teach pacing: the 62->73->50->62 guided demo is ~3 short moves (~6-7 s total). Confirm on the bench that three quick caps-stay-safe nudges feel informative and not like a long preamble before the scanner; if it drags, compress to a single up-then-down sweep.
